Introduction back to ToC

The POSTDATA Network of Ontologies enables the standardization of European Poetry by defining models that represent several concepts across different literary traditions, periods, poetic genres and authors. This enables the integration and exchange of poetry data as Linked Data among traditions and facilitates the retrieval of different aspects of Poetic Works, which helps to carry out relevant research in Comparative Literature and Comparative Metrical Studies in a quantitative fashion.

The core ontology of the network described hereafter (postdata-core) focuses on general and bibliographical characteristics of poems, plays and songs. It also defines classes that are not specific to the domain of poetry but are required to provide a complete description of poetic works, such as Persons, Organisations, Events and Places, while other features of the poetic works like prosodic, rhetorical devices, or textual transmission aspects are represented in the rest of ontologies of the network.

The core-ontology must be imported by the rest of ontologies in the network. For this reason, besides containing the mentioned classes, it also provides a set of common properties that have the same semantics in all the classes in which they are defined. In this way, it is possible to express semantics in an unambiguous way for properties that from this point of view conceptually represent the same.
